# Oracle Question Bank

Use these prompts as expansion tools, not a restrictive checklist. You do not need to ask everything, but you should default toward collecting more useful context rather than less.

## Universal Questions

- What, specifically, do you want the Oracle to produce?
- What would a successful answer let you do next?
- Should the answer optimize for breadth, depth, or both?
- Who is the audience, and what background should the answer assume?
- What constraints matter: time, budget, tooling, risk, citations, geography, timeframe, or format?
- What information, artifacts, or prior work should the Oracle treat as ground truth?
- What background led up to this request?
- What have you already tried, and what was unsatisfying about the results?
- What nearby details might seem secondary but could still help the Oracle reason better?
- What should the Oracle avoid doing?

## Context Expansion Prompts

- What else do you think I should know before handing this to the Oracle?
- Are there documents, screenshots, links, transcripts, notes, or examples that would help?
- What constraints or preferences often get forgotten when people answer this kind of question?
- Are there stakeholders, downstream decisions, or consequences tied to the answer?
- Are there competing interpretations, uncertainties, or tensions that should be preserved in the brief?
- What context feels messy, ambiguous, or hard to summarize but may still matter?

## Debugging and Technical Diagnosis

- What is the exact symptom or failure?
- What did you expect to happen, and what happened instead?
- Can you reproduce the issue consistently? If so, how?
- What is the environment: language, framework, OS, runtime, versions, infrastructure?
- What changed recently?
- What logs, stack traces, error messages, traces, or metrics are available?
- Which files, functions, services, or modules seem relevant?
- What fixes or hypotheses have already been ruled out?
- What architecture, deployment, or operational context would help explain the issue?
- What is the impact: severity, frequency, blast radius, user pain, or business consequence?
- Do you want likely root causes only, or a full investigation plan with ranked hypotheses?

## Research and Knowledge Synthesis

- What exact topic or question should the Oracle address?
- Do you want an overview, a deep dive, or both?
- What time horizon matters?
- Is the scope global or limited to a geography, population, industry, or domain?
- What level should the answer target: beginner, practitioner, expert, or researcher?
- Should the Oracle privilege textbooks, recent papers, industry guidance, regulatory sources, or practical heuristics?
- Should it surface controversies, competing schools of thought, and unresolved questions?
- Do you want a structured map first, recommendations first, or a linear narrative?
- What prior knowledge, assumptions, or source material should the Oracle build on?
- Should the Oracle aim for exhaustive coverage, decision usefulness, teaching clarity, or some combination?

## Planning, Design, and Decisions

- What decision or design problem are you trying to solve?
- What are the primary goals and non-goals?
- What hard constraints exist?
- What tradeoffs matter most?
- What options are already on the table?
- What is the timeline and what resources are available?
- Who are the stakeholders and what incentives or concerns do they have?
- What real-world constraints, politics, or implementation realities might affect the answer?
- Should the Oracle recommend one path, compare alternatives, or produce a phased plan?

## Writing, Explanation, and Communication

- What form should the output take: memo, brief, article, presentation, email, FAQ, or something else?
- Who is the target audience?
- What tone, length, and structure do you want?
- Are there examples to emulate or avoid?
- What points are mandatory, optional, or forbidden?
- What source material, notes, or background should be incorporated directly?
- What action should the writing cause the reader to take?
- Should the Oracle prioritize persuasion, clarity, completeness, technical precision, or brevity?

## Stop Asking When

- You know the task, audience, and success criteria.
- The major constraints are explicit.
- The evidence and surrounding background are already richly captured.
- Additional questions would mostly repeat information rather than uncovering new context.
